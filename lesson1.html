LESSON 1:

Introduction to Computing


Computer Science is an academic discipline that involves theoretical and practical aspects of computing, focusing on the study of algorithms and data structures, computer and network design, development of software and hardware systems, and artificial intelligence.



Introduction

It derives intellectual processes from other disciplines such as:


Theory

derived from Mathematics

Building conceptual frameworks and
notations to understand relationships
among subjects

- (definition) characterize objects of study
- (theorem) hypothesize possible relationships
- (proof) verification of relationships
- Interpret the results

Abstraction / Experimentation 

derived from Natural Sciences

Identifying patterns and distilling models
to emphasize the common characteristics from
case to case. To use those relationships
and make predictions

- Hypothesize 
- Creating a model and prediction
- Experiment design and data collection
- Analysis of result

Design

derived from Engineering

Interrelated with the processes of theory
and abstraction, with the aim to understand
the nature of computing rather than to create
a product. Implement specific instances of
those relationships to construct systems
to solve problems

- State requirements
- State specifications
- Design and implementation of system
- Testing the system

Computing Disciplines & Majors

Computer Engineering

- Development of software and hardware systems
- Design and construction of computers and computer-based systems
- Study of hardware, software, communications, and interaction among them
- Example: they design 3D printers, cell phones, robots,
control systems, and other digital devices

Key Skills:
- Embedded systems
- Digital systems design
- Computer architecture and organization
- Information security

Software Engineering

- Developing and maintaining large software systems
- Focus on software reliability to can cater to users

Key Skills:
- Mathematical and engineering
- Programming
- Software modeling and analysis
- Software design and processes
- Software security
- Software project management
- Data structures and algorithms

Information Technology

- Deploying and supporting the computing infrastructure in organizations
- Provide customer service with a focus on technology
- Focus on how to configure, use, and support technology
infrastructures within organizations
- Also develop websites and mobile apps
- Improve an organization’s data structure

Key Skills:
- Software development
- Networking management
- Cybersecurity
- Database management
- Project management
- System administration and maintenance

Information Systems

- Integrating information technology solutions to aid business processes
- Interface between the end user and technical experts
- Use technology as an instrument for generating, processing,
and distributing information

1. Executives - Executive Information System
2. Senior Managers - Decision Support System
3. Middle Managers - Management Information System
4. Workers - Transaction Processing Systems

Key Skills:
- Data and information management
- Enterprise architecture
- Systems analysis and design
- Knowledge management and business intelligence systems
- Project management

Computer Science

Covers the widest range of computing. Emphasizes theoretical foundations of
computing and development of new computing technologies and techniques

1. Devise new ways to use computers
2. Design and implement software
3. Develop effective ways to solve computing problems

Key Skills:
- Programming
- Algorithms and Data structures
- Graphics and visualization
- Human-computer interaction
- Artificial intelligence
- Operating systems
